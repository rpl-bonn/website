Recent advances in 2D/3D foundation models have enabled 4D spatial AI systems that can detect, ground, track, and build rich scene graphs from live perception. However, these systems treat every session independently and run the same models at the same intensity regardless of the scene — wasting compute in easy settings and failing in hard ones, while discarding all spatial knowledge between sessions. This thesis builds an **adaptive lifelong 4D spatial AI system** that solves both problems through a single unified mechanism: a memory-aware perceptual controller that dynamically adjusts what gets processed based on what is already known, while continuously compressing and storing new observations into a persistent hierarchical memory. Thus system perceives efficiently *because* it remembers, and remembers efficiently *because* it perceives adaptively — improving over time rather than resetting every session.
